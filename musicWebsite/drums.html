Drums

The drum is a member of the percussion group of musical
instruments. Drums consist of at least one membrane,
called a drumhead or drum skin, that is stretched over a
shell and struck, either directly with the player's hands,
or with a percussion mallet, to produce sound.

Drums have been found in Neolithic cultures located in
China, dating to a period of 5500–2350 BC. In literary
records, drums manifested shamanistic characteristics and
were often used in ritual ceremonies

In popular music and jazz, "drums" usually refers to a
drum kit or a set of drums
