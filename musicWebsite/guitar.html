Guitar

The guitar is a fretted musical instrument that typically has six strings.
Guitars can be divided into two broad categories, acoustic and electric
guitars.Within each of these categories, there are also further sub-categories.

Before the development of the electric guitar and the use of synthetic
materials, a guitar was defined as being an instrument having "a long,
fretted neck, flat wooden soundboard, ribs, and a flat back, with incurved sides."

Accoustic Guitars:

They form several notable subcategories within the acoustic guitar group:
classical and flamenco guitars; steel-string guitars, which include the
flat-topped, or "folk", guitar; twelve-string guitars; and the arched-top guitar.

The acoustic guitar group also includes unamplified guitars designed to play
in different registers, such as the acoustic bass guitar, which has a similar
tuning to that of the electric bass guitar.

Electric Guitars

They can have solid, semi-hollow, or hollow bodies; solid bodies produce little
sound without amplification. In contrast to a standard acoustic guitar,
electric guitars instead rely on electromagnetic pickups.

The sound is frequently modified by other electronic devices.The electric guitar
is used extensively in jazz, blues, R & B, and rock and roll.
