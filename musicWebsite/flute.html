Flute

The flute is a family of musical instruments in the woodwind group.A flute is an aerophone or reedless wind instrument that produces its sound from the flow of air across an opening.flutes are categorized as edge-blown aerophones.

A number of flutes dating to about 43,000-35,000 years ago have been found in the Swabian Jura region of present-day Germany.
These flutes demonstrate that a developed musical tradition existed from the earliest period of modern human presence in Europe.
Asia too has a long history with the instrument that has continued into the present day.

Indian Flute:

The bamboo flute is an important instrument in Indian classical music, and developed independently of the Western flute. The Hindu God Lord Krishna is traditionally considered a master of the bamboo flute. The Indian flutes are very simple compared to the Western counterparts; they are made of bamboo and are keyless

Chineese Flute:

In China there are many varieties of dizi,or Chinese flute, with different sizes, structures and number of holes and intonations .One peculiar feature of the Chinese flute is the use of a resonance membrane mounted on one of the holes that vibrates with the air column inside the tube. This membrane is called a di mo, which is usually a thin tissue paper. It gives the flute a bright sound

The Korean flute, called the daegeum,is a large bamboo transverse flute used in traditional Korean music. It has a buzzing membrane that gives it a unique timbre.
