Cello

The cello is a bowed string instrument of the violin family.
Its four strings are usually tuned in perfect fifths: from low
to high. As a solo instrument, the cello uses its whole range
from bass to soprano.

The earliest depictions of the violin family, from northern Italy
c. 1530. Contrary to a popular misconception, the cello did not
evolve from the viola da gamba, but existed alongside it
for about two and a half centuries.

Cellos are part of the standard symphony orchestra, which
includes eight to twelve cellists.The cellos are a critical part
of orchestral music; all symphonic works involve the cello
section, and many pieces require cello soli or solos.

The cello is typically made from carved wood.Laminated cellos
are widely used in elementary and secondary school orchestras
and youth orchestras, because they are much more durable,less expensive.
